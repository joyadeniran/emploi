# Writing Style Rules (mandatory)

## Hard rules
1. NO em-dashes. Use commas, periods, or restructure.
2. NO clichés or filler: "I am passionate about", "great fit", "leverage my skills", "hit the ground running", "drive results", "synergies", "dynamic", "fast-paced environment".
3. NO buzzwords without concrete backing. Every claim needs a specific example or fact from the candidate profile.
4. NO apologetic language. Not "I think I could contribute" but "I bring X, demonstrated by Y."
5. NO invented company facts. If a company claim isn't in the job description, phrase it generally or omit it.
6. Reframe emphasis, not substance. Apply the interview backtrack test: if the candidate would have to say "well, what I actually meant was..." in an interview, the claim is too far. Never claim experience the candidate doesn't have.

## Tone
Warm but direct. Conversational professional, like a confident person in a good interview. First person, active voice ("I built", not "a system was developed"). Demonstrate, don't state: instead of "I am a team player", give a specific teamwork example with its outcome.

## Cover letter structure
- Opening: state the role, connect background to it in 1-2 sentences. Specific to this company, never a template opener.
- Early "why this company" section: use themes from the posting; focus on contributing to their goals, not what the candidate gains.
- Body: forward-looking. Which of THEIR tasks can the candidate solve, and how? Methods, tools, expected outcomes. Use 1-2 brief past examples only as evidence for forward-looking claims. The letter is not a CV repetition.
- 3-5 concrete outcome-oriented bullets where appropriate; vary bullet structure; numbers and tools beat adjectives.
- Closing: brief, confident. "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss..." No begging, no over-enthusiasm.
- One page maximum. Scannable. Match the language of the job posting.

## Headline
Subject line formula: [candidate specialty] + [relevant keyword from the posting]. Never "Application for X Position".
