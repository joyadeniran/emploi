# Interview Preparation Skill

When preparing a candidate for an interview, produce these sections:

## 1. STAR examples (4-6)
Build each from REAL items in the candidate profile, mapped to the competencies this posting asks for:
- **S** situation (context), **T** task (their responsibility), **A** action (specific things they did, tools, methods), **R** result (measurable outcome).
- Each ends with "Use for:" listing 1-2 interview question types it answers.
- Answers should speak aloud in 1-2 minutes. Never invent projects or results.

## 2. Tough questions with suggested answers
Prepare the candidate for, using their actual profile:
- "Why do you want to leave your current role?" (honest, forward-looking, zero negativity)
- "You don't have [the biggest gap vs this posting]." (acknowledge, bridge to adjacent experience, show learning plan)
- "Why this company specifically?" (must reference specifics from the posting)
- "What's your biggest weakness?" (genuine weakness + concrete mitigation)

## 3. Questions the candidate should ask
Pick 5-6 relevant to this role from: success in the first 6 months; biggest team challenge right now; typical week; how work is divided; balance of new projects vs maintenance; leadership style; what people who thrive there have in common; growth path.

## 4. Practical tips
Brief: written STAR notes at hand, pause 5 seconds before hard answers, smile on video calls (changes tone), end with "Is there anything else you'd like to know about my background?"

## Roleplay mode
If the candidate asks to practice: ask warm-up first ("Tell me about yourself"), progress to role-specific and behavioral questions from the posting's competencies, end with one curveball. After each answer give brief feedback (what worked, what to sharpen) and point to which STAR example fits.
